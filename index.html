KFC was my first job, at which I worked for 3 years during high school and the summer after my first year of college. While all those long and cozy nights spent slinging the Colonel's chicken were extremely facinating and a good learning experience I had bigger dreams and asperations in my life. So as I bounced from job to job during the summer breaks of college, I was finally graduating from college and ready to start my career, only the world wasn't ready for me. The only job I could find being a political science major was working in the shoe department at Macy's. While this wasn't a dream job it paid the bills and gave me a great opportunity to develop an unusual hobby for a woman my age, collecting shoes. I mean 50% off discount and first dibs on every style in your size! Unfortunate this "fun" all came to an end when I was hired for a new position which resulted in a new city and a significant income increase. Insert the worst job ever.

VS

No one ever can really tell you how bad working at a call center can be, it seems like something you really must experience for yourself. 5 years it took me to not only recognize how unhappy answering phones made me and to do something about it During these 5 years I decided to also get a second job as a bartender which I still work at today. While I don't get to bartend as much anymore I still enjoy the face to face interaction with my guests, and the stories I get to witness as the weirdest things happen in a bar. To top it off, the bar I work in is at a movie theater. If you have ever people watched at a movie theater you probably understand my enjoyment. If you haven't you should because every type of person in the world goes to a movie every now and then, and it's one of those experiences you just can't miss. You'll have to ask me sometime as I have a quite the collection of stories from over the past 3 years that will leave your in tears with laughter. While all this is going on I discover a new career path with a start up company. If you have ever worked for a start up then you know how challenging it can be, in the best possible ways. Here I was able to Discover myself more in the work environment, learn my weakness, my strengths, my likes, and dislikes. In the past the path has always been so clear cut, this is what we hired you to do, so this is what you are going to do. Here I was playing a whole new ball game with a different set of rules and a path I could pave for myself. Which is what brought me to the Sprint Accelerator every Monday and Thursday night!
