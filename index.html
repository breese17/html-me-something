KFC

KFC was my first job, at which I worked for 3 years during high school and the summer after my first year of college. While all those long and cozy nights spent slinging the Colonel's chicken were extremely facinating and a good learning experience I had bigger dreams and asperations in my life. So as I bounced from job to job during the summer breaks of college, I was finally graduating from college and ready to start my career, only the world wasn't ready for me. The only job I could find being a political science major was working in the shoe department at Macy's. While this wasn't a dream job it paid the bills and gave me a great opportunity to develop an unusual hobby for a woman my age, collecting shoes. I mean 50% off discount and first dibs on every style in your size! Unfortunate this "fun" all came to an end when I was hired for a new position which resulted in a new city and a significant income increase. Insert the worst job ever.

No one ever can really tell you how bad a job can be it seems like something you really must experience for yourself. 5 years it took me to not only recognize how bad this position was but to also do something about it During these 5 years I decided to also get a second job as a bartender which I still work at today. While I don't get to bartend as much anymore I still enjoy the face to face interaction with my guests, and the stories I get to witness as the weirdest things happen in a bar. To top it off, the bar I work in is at a movie theater. If you have ever people watched at a movie theater you probably understand my enjoyment. If you haven't you should because every type of person in the world goes to a movie every now and then, and it's one of those experiences you just can't miss.
